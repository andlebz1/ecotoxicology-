About the Lab

Understanding pollutants, at the level of genes and communities

The Ecotoxicology Lab investigates how environmental pollutants — from waterborne heavy metals to excess fluoride in groundwater — interact with human biology and shape long‑term health outcomes. Our work sits at the intersection of environmental toxicology and molecular biology, combining field‑based exposure assessment with laboratory‑based genetic analysis.

We study biomarkers of exposure and susceptibility, apply PCR‑based genotyping to identify genetic variants associated with pollutant sensitivity, and evaluate the downstream effects of contaminants such as fluoride and heavy metals on children and vulnerable populations. Our findings are intended to inform public health policy, water quality standards, and community‑level risk mitigation.

By linking environmental data, molecular genetics, and epidemiology, the lab aims to generate evidence that is both scientifically rigorous and practically useful for underserved and environmentally exposed communities.

What We Study

Research Areas

Seven interconnected areas that shape our understanding of environmental exposure and human health.

🌱

Environmental Toxicology

Examining how pollutants move through ecosystems and accumulate in living organisms over time.

🦷

Fluoride Exposure Studies

Assessing groundwater fluoride levels and their association with dental and skeletal fluorosis.

⚗️

Heavy Metal Toxicity

Investigating exposure pathways and physiological effects of arsenic, lead, cadmium, and related metals.

🧬

Molecular Biomarkers

Identifying biological indicators of pollutant exposure, susceptibility, and early health effects.

🩺

Human Health Risk Assessment

Translating environmental and genetic data into evidence‑based public health risk evaluations.

🧪

Genotyping & PCR‑Based Analysis

Using Tetra‑ARMS PCR and related molecular techniques to characterize gene variants of interest.

💧

Water Quality & Environmental Monitoring

Field sampling and analysis of groundwater and surface water in exposed communities.

Lab Projects

Ongoing and upcoming research initiatives — get in touch if you'd like to contribute.

Ongoing

Assessment of Fluoride Exposure in Children

Evaluating groundwater fluoride levels and dental fluorosis prevalence among children in fluoride‑endemic regions, linked with VDR gene variability.

Ongoing

Genetic Susceptibility & Environmental Pollutant Exposure

Investigating gene–environment interactions that influence individual susceptibility to pollutant‑related health outcomes.

Completed

Water Quality Analysis in Exposed Communities

Field‑based sampling and laboratory analysis of groundwater quality in communities with known contaminant exposure.

Future

Biomarker‑Based Evaluation of Toxic Effects

Developing a panel of molecular biomarkers to evaluate early physiological effects of pollutant exposure in at‑risk populations.
